Credits

Jez Hill and Jason Farquhar dreamed up the idea of linking BCI2000 to VisionEgg in order to provide a rapid development environment for BCI experiments.

Masters student Thomas Schreiner, supervised principally by Jason, succeeded in realizing this dream far more thoroughly and elegantly than we had ever imagined. By the time he submitted his Diploma thesis in April 2008, the BCPy2000 application module was already in use in a real patient BCI project, and already fulfilling its promise of increasing flexibility and saving a great deal of development time.

Since Thomas moved on to better-paid things, Jez and research assistant Christian Puzicha have given the BCPy2000 the philosopher's-axe treatment a couple of times over, to bring it from a beta to a releasable state, and expand it to enable python-based signal processing and test-signal generation. However, the system still owes its form and philosophy to Thomas and Jason's work. It is currently maintained by Jez.

We're grateful for the beta-testing efforts of Mike Bensch and Bernd Battes, both of whom were thrown into the murky undocumented depths. Many thanks also to Jürgen Mellinger and Gerwin Schalk for hosting the project, and for their help, advice and encouragement in integrating our work with the BCI2000.

During development we've been supported by the Max-Planck-Gesellschaft in Prof. Bernhard Schölkopf's department of the Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics, and by the Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft under SFB550.
