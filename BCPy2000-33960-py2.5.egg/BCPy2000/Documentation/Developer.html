Developer

In its current form, BCPy2000 is aimed at developers â€” people who can (or are prepared to learn to) program in python.

Developers of BCPy2000 application modules are likely to be, or to work directly for, scientists who are developing new experimental techniques and induction methods for BCI. Your experiments constantly require new stimulus arrangements and behaviours that are not available in out-of-the-box BCI2000 binaries, and you find implementing these in C++ to be longwinded and tedious. VisionEgg and the BCPy2000 phase machine are going to be your friends.

Developers of BCPy2000 signal-processing modules are likely to be specialists in signal-processing and/or machine-learning who are experimenting with new algorithmic approaches. You aren't afraid of three-letter acronyms like ICA, CSP or SVM. You probably have some experience with Matlab and have possibly used the BCI2000 MatlabSignalProcessing module, but you're interested in migrating to python or want to use your existing python code. You rarely trust anybody's analytical implementation except your own. scipy.signal and numpy.linalg are likely to be your constant companions.

BCPy2000 allows you as developer to implement a new source, signal-processing or application module by defining a single python class in the appropriate developer file.
